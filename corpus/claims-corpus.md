## Document 1: Prior Authorization Policy — Physical Therapy Services (PA-101)
**Effective Date:** Jan 1, 2026  
**Applies To:** Commercial PPO, HSA Plans  

### Section 1 — Overview
Certain outpatient physical therapy (PT) services require **prior authorization (PA)** to ensure appropriate utilization and medical necessity.

### Section 2 — Services Requiring PA
- CPT **97110** — Therapeutic Exercise  
- CPT **97112** — Neuromuscular Re-education  
- CPT **97530** — Therapeutic Activities  

### Section 3 — PA Exemptions
PA is **NOT required** for:
- Initial evaluation (CPT 97161–97163)  
- First **6 visits** for post-surgical rehab within 45 days  

### Section 4 — Required Documentation
- Initial evaluation / functional deficit  
- Treatment goals  
- Expected duration  
- Prior therapy history  

### Section 5 — Decision Timeframe
All PA requests are processed within **2 business days**.

## Document 2: Coverage Policy — Durable Medical Equipment (CP-204)
**Applies To:** All commercial products  

### Covered When Medically Necessary
- Standard wheelchairs  
- CPAP devices  
- TENS units (after 30-day home trial)  

### Not Covered
- Exercise equipment  
- Bath/shower benches  
- Lift chairs (non-medical components)  

### Documentation Needed
- Prescription  
- Letter of medical necessity  
- Trial results when applicable

## Document 3: Medical Necessity Criteria — Imaging (MN-330)
### MRI of the Lumbar Spine — Medically necessary when:
1. **Persistent pain > 6 weeks** despite conservative therapy  
2. Documented **neurologic deficit**  
3. Suspected **tumor, infection, or fracture**  

### CT Abdomen/Pelvis — Medically necessary when:
- Suspected appendicitis or bowel obstruction  
- Abnormal ultrasound requiring further evaluation

## Document 4: Claims Submission Guidelines (CS-001)
### Timely Filing Limit
Claims must be submitted within **90 days** of the date of service.

### Required Claim Fields
- Member ID  
- Provider NPI  
- CPT/HCPCS  
- Diagnosis ICD-10  
- Place of service  
- Date of service  

### Missing Info Examples (Likely Denials)
- Missing NPI  
- Incomplete diagnosis  
- Invalid CPT combination

## Document 5: Denials & Appeals Policy (DN-050)
### Common Denial Reasons
- No prior authorization  
- Lack of medical necessity  
- Non-covered service  
- Out-of-network provider without referral  

### Appeal Levels
1. **Level 1 — Internal Review** (15 days)  
2. **Level 2 — Clinical Review** (30 days)  
3. **External Review** when applicable  

### Appeal Submission Must Include:
- Medical records  
- Provider rationale  
- Corrected claim (if applicable)

## Document 6: CPT-Specific Policy — CPT 97110 (PT-97110-SPEC)
### When Covered
- Conditions impacting strength, flexibility, endurance  
- Post-operative recovery programs  
- Functional impairments documented in the evaluation  

### When Prior Authorization Is Required
- **All non-post-surgical cases** beyond 6 initial visits  
- Cases with high utilization (>20 visits in 60 days)  

### Documentation Needed
- Objective measures: ROM, strength, gait  
- Goals tied to functional improvement  
- Progress notes every 6 visits
